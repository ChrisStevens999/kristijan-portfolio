# CONTENT ARCHITECTURE

## Purpose

This document defines how information is organized throughout the portfolio.

It does not define the content itself.

Content may change over time.

The structure should remain consistent.

---

# Homepage

The homepage is a guided experience.

Visitors should discover the portfolio in the following order:

1. Intro
2. Selected Works
3. Explore by Category
4. About
5. Contact

Each section has a single purpose.

No section should compete with another.

---

# Intro

Purpose

Create curiosity.

Introduce the designer emotionally before presenting work.

The intro should always transition naturally into the homepage.

---

# Selected Works

Purpose

Present only the strongest work.

Selected Works is a curated collection.

It is not a complete archive.

Projects should be replaced whenever stronger work is created.

Selection is based on quality rather than chronology.

---

# Explore by Category

Purpose

Allow visitors to explore creative disciplines.

Categories function as chapters rather than filters.

Each category should immediately communicate its creative identity through imagery.

---

# Project Pages

Purpose

Tell the complete story behind a project.

Each project page should answer:

• What was the challenge?

• What was the idea?

• How was it developed?

• What was the final outcome?

Projects should prioritize visual storytelling over long paragraphs.

---

# About

Purpose

Introduce the person behind the work.

Focus on philosophy, process and personality.

Avoid a traditional résumé format.

---

# Contact

Purpose

Provide a simple way to connect.

The visitor should never need to search for contact information.

---

# Navigation

Navigation should remain minimal.

The portfolio should encourage exploration without overwhelming the visitor.

Navigation exists to support the experience rather than dominate it.

---

# Future Growth

The portfolio should be designed to grow naturally.

New projects, categories or pages should integrate into the existing structure without requiring a redesign.
