# CREATIVE MANIFESTO

## Why I Create

I believe design should leave an impression before it delivers information.

People rarely remember every detail of a project, but they always remember how it made them feel.

That belief guides every decision I make.

Whether I am designing a brand, an apparel collection, an illustration or an experimental concept, my goal is always the same:

Create something people remember.

---

## My Approach

I don't separate strategy from aesthetics.

Strong visuals without meaning become decoration.

Strong strategy without emotion becomes forgettable.

The most memorable work exists where concept and execution become inseparable.

---

## Restraint

Not every space needs to be filled.

Not every animation needs to exist.

Not every project needs to be shown.

The confidence to remove is often more valuable than the desire to add.

---

## Storytelling

Every project tells a story.

The website itself tells another story.

Neither should explain everything immediately.

Curiosity is part of the experience.

Discovery creates memory.

---

## Originality

I don't chase trends.

I study them.

Then I build something that feels personal.

Inspiration is welcomed.

Imitation is avoided.

---

## Craft

Details matter.

Typography matters.

Spacing matters.

Image selection matters.

Transitions matter.

Every decision contributes to the final experience.

There are no small details.

Only overlooked ones.

---

## Growth

This portfolio is never finished.

It is an evolving archive of ideas, experiments and professional work.

As I grow, the portfolio grows with me.

The system should be flexible enough to evolve without losing its identity.

---

## Final Principle

If a creative decision makes the portfolio feel more memorable, more intentional and more human, it is usually the right decision.
