# DECISION LOG

## Purpose

This document records important decisions made during the development of the portfolio.

The goal is not to document every change.

The goal is to preserve the reasoning behind significant creative, UX, technical and strategic decisions.

Future decisions should reference this log before changing existing systems.

---

## Format

Date

Decision

Reason

Result (optional)

---

# 2026-07-16

## Decision

The homepage follows this structure:

Intro

↓

Selected Works

↓

Explore by Category

↓

About

↓

Contact

### Reason

The experience should introduce emotion first, showcase the strongest work second, provide exploration third, then introduce the designer before offering contact information.

This creates a narrative rather than a traditional portfolio layout.

---

# 2026-07-16

## Decision

The homepage begins with an immersive intro sequence.

### Reason

The intro establishes mood and identity before presenting work.

The visitor should remember the experience before remembering individual projects.

---

# 2026-07-16

## Decision

Selected Works is a curated collection rather than a complete archive.

### Reason

Quality creates stronger perception than quantity.

The homepage should always represent the best work available at that time.

---

# 2026-07-16

## Decision

Categories function as chapter covers instead of portfolio filters.

### Reason

The goal is exploration.

Each category should feel like entering another room in an exhibition.

---

# 2026-07-16

## Decision

Typography remains HTML rather than being embedded inside imagery.

### Reason

Improves accessibility, responsiveness and future editing while preserving the visual direction.

---

# 2026-07-16

## Decision

Documentation is completed before development begins.

### Reason

The portfolio should be built intentionally rather than evolving through random implementation decisions.

The documentation becomes the project's source of truth.

---

# Future Entries

Record only meaningful decisions.

Avoid documenting minor implementation details.

Focus on decisions that affect the identity, structure or long-term evolution of the portfolio.
